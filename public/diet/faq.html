Overview

What is Alzheimer's Disease?
Alzheimer's is the most common form of dementia in which generalized degeneration of the brain causes problems with memory, thinking and behavior. Symptoms usually develop slowly and get worse over time, becoming severe enough to interfere with daily tasks.
How common is Alzheimer's Disease?
An estimated 5.4 million Americans of all ages have Alzheimer's disease in 2016. Of the 5.4 million Americans with Alzheimer's, an estimated 5.2 million people are age 65 and older, and approximately 200,000 individuals are under age 65 (younger-onset Alzheimer's). This number is projected to triple by the year 2050.
What is the MIND Diet?
"The MIND diet, short for the Mediterranean-DASH Intervention for Neurodegenerative Delay diet, has been directly linked to a significant improvement in neurological function. What does this mean for you? Through an extensive research trial, scientists at Rush University Medical Center found that following this diet can reduce your risk of Alzheimer's and dementia by up to 53%!  Even moderately following the diet reduces your risk by over a third. The diet focuses on 10 brain-healthy foods including leafy greens, nuts and limits potentially damaging foods like red meat, pastries, and fried foods. In addition to reducing incidence of Alzheimer’s disease, following the MIND diet has also been linked to lower risk of cardiovascular conditions like hypertension, heart attack and stroke."
Is there research to back the MIND Diet's claim on reducing risk of dementia and Alzheimer's?
Yes. The MIND Trial was conducted by Dr. Morris and her team in which over 900 Chicago area seniors were followed over an average of 4.5 years. A comprehensive questionnaire was used to collect data on participant dietary patterns and yearly cognitive tests were performed. The study found the MIND diet lowered Alzheimer’s risk by about 35% amongst those who followed it moderately well and up to 53% for those who stricktly adhered to the diet. Other large studies have also found significant links between the consumption of specific MIND Diet foods like leafy greens and brain health.

ABOUT PROGRAM

What is the Brainsalvation Dietary Program?
The Brainsalvation Dietary Program is the first of its kind, designed to help women reduce their risk of developing dementia and Alzheimer's disease using the scientifically proven principles of the MIND diet. With the guidance and support of Brainsalvation’s expert Nutrition Consultants and Health Coaches, program participants will implement dietary modifications focused on achieving optimal brain health. While our primary focus is to reduce the risk of Alzheimer’s disease, feedback and coaching will be tailored to meet individual needs and help users achieve their personal health and wellness goals.
How much does the Brainsalvation Dietary Program cost?
The program enrollment fee is $149 which includes the initial consultation with a Brainsalvation Nutrition Consultant and 3 months of nutrition coaching. This also includes a MIND Diet recipe E-book, and a montly progress report. After the initial 3 month period the monthly rate is only $45.
What qualifications do Brainsalvation's Nutrition Consultants have?
Nutrition Consultants are certified Registered Dietitian Nutritionists with a minimum of 4 years of experience in the field.
What qualifications do Brainsalvation's Health Coaches have?
Health Coaches hold a minimum of a bachelor's degree in nutrition or 2 health related degrees plus 1 nutrition related coaching certification. All coaches have passed Pegara's rigorous selection process.

When is the starting date of subscription? (the date of application, the date of appointment with healthcoach or the date of consultation with NC)
You can begin the 90-day program anytime you like.  Within 24 hours of enrollment, you will 1.) receive email with instructions on how to complete a 3-day  photographic food record and 2.) will be connected with a nutrition consultant. Your consultant will schedule a phone or Skype nutrition consultation with you at this time.  We offer great flexibility with the scheduling of the consultation, as we know how busy our clients are. After your 3-day food record has been received, and you complete the phone or skype nutrition consultantion, the 90 day coaching program offically begins!  Within one day of your nutrition consultation, you should receive a greeting text message from your health coach.  You will communicate via text with your coach on a daily basis. Your coach will respond to you at least 5 days each week. The number of responses you receive each of those 5 days will vary based on a variety of factors.
